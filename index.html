Who Am I?

Download Resume

My name is River Hoffman, I am currently a college student and a driven Front-End Developer; born and raised in the great city of New York. From a young age, I have been inspired to create; I dabbled in plenty of art forms: music, photography, and videography etc. I became intrigued with website design and development once I entered college. There I mastered the front-end programming languages: HTML, CSS, Javascript, and JQuery, but also became equipped to use the programs on the Adobe Creative Suite, so I could comprehend the production of content.

As of May of 2019, I will be graduating from Rochester Institute of Technology, with a Bachelors of Science in Applied Arts & Science with a Minor in Chinese. I am currently looking for a full-time position where I can flourish and be a part of a creative team, making an impact on the world.

This website is a portfolio of my previous work, which I tend to update on a monthly basis and a way for interested people (like yourself) to get in contact with me.

Projects

CMDS 510 | Multilingual Website

For my capstone project, I decided to focus on RIT’s online publications; I realized they were only produced in English. What about the other popular languages that are prominent on the Internet? I created a mock website through Drupal 8 for one of the school’s news publications with the function to translate entire articles from English to Simplified Chinese, which would then be available to the reader.

Servu Solutions | Personality Quiz [Demo]

Created an algorithm that matches individuals with specific organizational cultures, based off their personality traits. For the demo, we focused on the bar industry in and around Rochester, NY

Digital JavaScript Clock | Just for Fun

Created a digital JavaScript clock that is accurate to the nearest second.

MLCH 449 | Digital Project

Created a website that focused on three of the Ancient Chinese Schools of Thought (Buddhism, Confucianism, and Taoism) using HTML5 and CSS3 and Javascript frameworks. In the website, there is a "Buzzfeed style" personality quiz (created by Interact), general information on the three schools of thought, and a list of suggested reading to learn more about the three ancient philosophies.

MAAT 301 | Marketing Campaign

Created a mock variable data marketing campaign to inform residents of the Brooklyn, NY area of the homeless population within their community and persuade them to volunteer to improve the lives of the homeless population in their community. Using datasets from the New York City Department of Homeless Services (DHS) personal- ized brochures, letters, postcards, and posters were created.

MAAT 383 | Magazine

Created two 8-paged magazines (digital and printed copies). Given free reign using Adobe Bridge, Illustrator, Indesign, and Photoshop, to create a magazine. The final product was a magazine that focused on ways to combat and control ones’ clinical depression.

CSCI 141 | HPI Database

Created a personal database of the home price index (HPI) of each state in the United States, had to implement several text files which were subsets in HPI databases by only using Python.

MAAT 272 | NYT Portal

Implemented the New York Times’ Application Programming Inter- face (API) into a webpage. Implementing the newspaper’s personal API, a search engine for articles about musicians, published by the NYT was created.
[Since 2017 the API for the NYT has changed significantly, making this website broken.]

MAAT 271 | Blog Redesign

Remodeled the Rochester Institute of Technology’s College of Imag- ing Arts & Sciences’ (CIAS) blog, a sample design was created using HTML 5 and CSS 3 frameworks and several image and text files provided by the CIAS previous blog posts.
